Embedded Google Maps

The blog post I chose the for my topic is I frame embedded Google Maps. I believe that this is one of the most interesting and useful web development tools for business. An iframe embedded Google map is an interactive window of a map provided by Google Maps gaps service embedded on your web page, allowing patrons of your website to better understand a location that is related to your website's common goal. As we already know, a frame was formerly known as an inline frame and is an element that allows you to add windows that embed other web pages or content on to different web pages. Today, most businesses rely on e-commerce. This dependence brings all users to websites that provide information to users to allow a functioning business to flourish. This information could be a service provided at a certain location or a way to distinguish different services by different locations.

It may be imperative that this information is provided to my clients or customers in a centralized location that is associated with my business. Putting extra work on the customer to go on the internet and search for my business location would be a bit unprofessional. Also, if I have a business with multiple locations, it may be easier for my customers to visit my website and know the location and address of what site provides what service. That is when iframe embedded Google map became a very important part of how we advertise the location that is related to a business or institution. This is done by simply going to googlemaps.com, searching for a location, clicking share and selecting the embed a map tab. This will provide you with a line of code for your HTML that can be either small, medium-large or one that has a custom ratio.

This tool allows developers the ability to take an embedded map of a certain location onto their web page for web developers to later style and make their own. For example, I work for a company that has multiple locations that all offer different services. We embed the Google Maps I frame in links that point to different locations that offer certain services to give users a better idea of what site works best for them. This is one of the many examples that make these embedded Google I frame useful for businesses connecting users who visit your website to your physical location. This is easily one of the most useful tools utilized by businesses today and is a way of inviting users who visit your website to your front door. I will be embedding a Google map that references a location to demonstrate how it works and style it to make it suitable for my web page. I also feel like this promotes retention, allowing users no need to go to any other website to will try to find directions to your service, as well as giving the customers the ability to decide whether traveling to this location is something they can deal with by checking out the street view while on your page.For my addition, i will be adding a Google map to my page with directions to RVCC.
